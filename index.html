01 / WHY THIS EXISTS

A lot is already moving. It just rarely becomes a circle.

I practice non-consumption: swapping instead of buying, creating instead of consuming, and looking for surplus resources already around us. My events, consultations and stays have often moved this way — but mostly one relationship at a time.

When there is no immediate one-to-one match, those exchanges can remain unresolved. Flow Circle makes existing exchange relationships visible as a network, so the people connected through it can also find, trust and support one another.

It carries a “Don’t Do It Yourself” spirit: a request can be a wish and an invitation to co-create. It also makes invisible labor easier to notice without turning kindness into wages, scores or an obligation to say yes.

NOTa bank
a leaderboard
a promise to serve everyone

06 / THE BOUNDARY

Care should be easier to offer — never harder to refuse.

01You can ask.

Needing help is not a failure or a debt.

02You can say no.

An offer is not an obligation, and refusal carries no penalty.

03You can leave it unrecorded.

Not all care needs to become data to be real.

04You can correct the story.

People involved can change, question or withdraw a record.
